## 1. Reward Mechanics — Beans

Separate system called "bean." Kids get physical dried black beans that go in a jar with their name on it. Elizabeth handles reimbursement. Simple rule: at any point during the day when a child reaches 100%, they get a bean. Beans are tracked and managed entirely outside the application for now — the app only needs to know when a kid reaches 100% and when they check off specific items.

## 2. Views / Screens

**Dashboard View (primary):** Shows everyone's to-do list in columns with items either not done or done. Done items have a checked checkbox, not-done items have an empty box. Checked-off items might be crossed out. At a glance, shows up to all six people's to-do lists in landscape mode. This is where the app lives most of the time.

**Single List View:** Tap one of the to-do lists on the dashboard and it zooms in / full-screens that list only. Tapping anywhere on an item toggles it done/not-done. Back to dashboard via X or back button in top left.

**Entry (within Single List View):** In single list view, you can talk to add items. Say "add item" or just describe items. E.g., "practice piano Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday" — AI parses free text into a recurring task behind the scenes. No Google Calendar-style recurring entry UI. Just natural language.

**Day Navigation:** On every screen (dashboard and single list), there's a forward-a-day / back-a-day mechanism to view previous days.

**Settings:** Add additional people to the list. Basic configuration.

**Streaks / Analytics:** A view showing people's completion over time. Secondary — most of the time the app stays in dashboard mode.

## 3. Saturday Rules

## 4. Roll-Over Behavior

No to-dos roll over. Everything starts fresh each day.
