# Principle - Cooperative Not Competitive

## WHAT: The Principle

Every motivation mechanic in WeDo should encourage personal completion and family mutual support — never compare family members against each other or create winners and losers.

## WHERE: Ecosystem

- Product Thesis:
  - [[Product Thesis - Cooperative Household]] — the thesis this principle operationalizes
- Standards:
  - [[Standard - Task Vocabulary]] — forbids competitive vocabulary (points, score, leaderboard) in any copy
  - [[Standard - Streak Calculation]] — streak mechanics are personal; no cross-person ranking
- Governs:
  - [[Domain - Daily Board]] — the entire shared board must express cooperative framing
  - [[Section - Dashboard View]] — column layout and completion display must not rank members
  - [[Section - Streaks and Analytics]] — streak display must be personal, not comparative
  - [[Loop - Daily Completion Rhythm]] — the daily loop is about household completion, not individual race
- Related:
  - [[Principle - Warmth Over Urgency]] — the emotional register that makes cooperation feel genuine

## WHEN: Timeline

Established at founding (v1) — the cooperative framing was baked into the product name and concept before any design decisions were made. It is unlikely to change as long as the household remains a cooperative unit with shared values around mutual support. It could be revisited if the children are significantly older and the family dynamic shifts toward wanting explicit competitive motivation — but that would require an explicit family conversation, not just a product decision. The specific mechanics (no ranking, personal-only streak display) are v1 implementations of this principle and may evolve; the principle itself is durable.

## WHY: Belief

The family's relational health depends on cooperation, not competition. Children in the same household who are ranked against each other develop sibling rivalry; parents who see a "worst performer" indicator develop a shame dynamic. The product should model the family as a unit working together, because that is what the family actually is — and what it aspires to be.

## HOW: Application

### What Following This Looks Like

- Completion rings show each person's own progress only — no side-by-side percentage comparison with labels
- Streak counts are visible on personal views but there is no "family leaderboard" or "who has the best streak" summary
- Celebrations (100% completion) feel like personal achievements that contribute to a family win, not individual trophies
- The physical bean reward system (external) is managed by parents, not surfaced competitively in-app

### What Violating This Looks Like

- A "top of the board" indicator showing who completed the most tasks this week
- Sorting family columns by completion rate (highest to lowest)
- "Moriah has done 3 more tasks than you today" style notifications
- Displaying comparative streak lengths in a ranking format

### Tensions

Trades off against: motivational effectiveness. Individual competition can be more viscerally motivating than cooperative framing. The family has explicitly chosen cooperation over maximum individual motivation — this is a values choice, not a pure optimization.

### Test

Ask: "Does this feature make a family member feel good about their own progress, or feel good because they're beating someone else?" If the latter, it violates this principle.
