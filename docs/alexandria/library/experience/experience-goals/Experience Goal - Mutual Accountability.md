# Experience Goal - Mutual Accountability

## WHAT: Definition

Mutual Accountability is the target social experience of WeDo's shared visibility model: because everyone's daily tasks are visible to the whole household, family members naturally notice each other's progress and offer encouragement — without the product needing to mediate, enforce, or report. The board creates accountability through presence, not through alerts or reminders.

## WHERE: Ecosystem

- Applies to sections:
  - [[Section - Dashboard View]] — the whole-family view is the mechanism for shared visibility
  - [[Section - Streaks and Analytics]] — streak history extends visibility across time
- Governed by:
  - [[Principle - Cooperative Not Competitive]] — accountability must be mutual and supportive, not comparative and judgmental
  - [[Principle - Warmth Over Urgency]] — the accountability feeling should be gentle ("I see you're almost done!") not pressuring ("You haven't done your tasks yet")
- Shapes loops:
  - [[Loop - Daily Completion Rhythm]] — shared visibility of rings and task lists creates gentle household accountability throughout the day
  - [[Loop - Streak Motivation]] — streak visibility across the family creates mutual awareness of each other's consistency

## WHY: Rationale

- Product Thesis: [[Product Thesis - Cooperative Household]] — the family's cooperative dynamic is enabled by shared visibility; each Person seeing the others' progress creates the "we" in WeDo
- The founder described wanting the family to "glance at it together." Mutual Accountability is the social mechanism behind that glance.

## WHEN: Timeline

V1 foundational experience goal. This goal is achieved through design (shared view) not features (reminders, reports).

## HOW: Implementation

### What Achieving This Looks Like

- A parent glances at the board and sees Micah's ring is only 20% at 4pm → mentions it casually in passing; Micah completes his tasks
- Moriah notices Wells' ring is full before hers and feels mild encouragement to finish her own (personal motivation, not competition)
- The family celebrates together when all six rings are full — no app mechanic required; the visual speaks for itself

### What Violating This Looks Like

- The app sending "Wells hasn't done his tasks" notifications — accountability should come from the board's visible presence, not from the app policing behavior
- Designing the board so that one Person's incomplete tasks are hidden from others — visibility is the mechanism; hiding defeats the purpose
- Making accountability feel like surveillance (e.g., timestamps showing when each task was completed)

### Tensions

Trades off against: privacy. Household members can see each other's tasks and completion state. This is intentional — but it requires the family to have trust and use the visibility supportively, not punitively. The product cannot enforce that; it can only design for warmth and hope the family matches.
